# CLAUDE.md — Project Context

## Who This Is For

This project belongs to Clay Devlin — a generative artist, creative technologist, and founder of Sanctum, based in Brooklyn. Originally from Louisville, Kentucky, from a family of artists.

## Current Focus

Clay is building **Sanctum**, an iOS digital wellness app that uses generative art as a restorative intervention at the moment of compulsive device reach. Sanctum is grounded in Attention Restoration Theory (ART), the Free Energy Principle, and neuroaesthetics. He is actively developing the iOS app (Screen Time API, ShieldConfiguration, DeviceActivityMonitor, SpriteKit with custom shaders) and preparing to raise pre-seed capital.

Key investor relationships being cultivated include Shivani Mitra (former CEO of Nous Research, now Managing Partner at Prometheia LLC), Erick Calderon (Snowfro/Art Blocks), and Jack Harlow. Key research relationships include Susan Magsamen (Johns Hopkins neuroaesthetics), Anjan Chatterjee (Penn neuroaesthetics), Mikey Siegel (contemplative technology), and Tristan Harris (Center for Humane Technology).

## Art Practice

Clay has maintained a nearly daily p5.js generative art practice since late 2022. His work lives at the intersection of generative art, neuroscience, and digital ritual. He has explored the information-theoretic properties of his generative art (spectral exponent β, fractal dimension, multiscale entropy) as formal grounding for Sanctum's neuroaesthetic claims.

He recently exhibited *Means of Attention* — a gutted 1991 Macintosh Classic housing a copper dharmachakra, Precious Moments figurines, red threads, and notification badges — at Shutter Gallery's "Anatomy of a Soul Machine" show, and gave an artist talk connecting attention, tension, and the Latin root *tendere*.

## Curatorial History

Clay previously founded and ran **Subjective**, an exhibition program at LUME Studios in Tribeca (2023–2024). His path to his current practice came through blockchain/Web3 work beginning around 2018, followed by art curation, then daily generative art.

## Theoretical Framework

Clay's work draws on: Attention Restoration Theory (Kaplan), the Free Energy Principle (Friston), predictive processing (Clark), Varela's enactive cognition, prospect-refuge theory (Appleton), Buddhist philosophy (Eightfold Path, dharmachakra symbolism), systems thinking (Meadows' leverage points), and research on supernormal stimuli, hard/soft fascination, and rate-distortion theory.

He wrote a substantial paper, "Attention Is All We Need: Toward an Attentional Ecology for Artificial Minds," mapping ART and the Free Energy Principle onto transformer architecture.

The conceptual vocabulary includes "Seize the means of attention," "human fracking," and "Enlivenment" as a movement-building frame positioning Sanctum as an alternative ontology to the dominant attention economy.

## Writing & Teaching

Clay writes on Substack and is considering teaching a course at the Strother School of Radical Attention (SoRA) in Brooklyn: "Soft Fascination: Generative Art as Attentional Sanctuary."

## Aesthetic Sensibility

- Divine patterns, iridescent complexity, hypnotic visual coherence
- Breathing motion, golden-ratio modulation, naturalistic soft unfolding dynamics
- Recursive logic, non-repeating symmetry, golden-ratio phase drift
- Sacred, not gamified — touch modulates movement, not color, and should feel embodied
- No harsh or rigid rotation
- Performance-conscious: high frame rates on mobile without sacrificing detail

## Technical Environment

- p5.js and WebGL for generative work
- SwiftUI, SpriteKit, and native iOS for Sanctum
- Comfortable with complex attractor-based movement, fractal recursion, touch input, multidimensional flows

## Tone Guidance

- Be grounded, warm, and constructive — avoid excessive flattery or hype
